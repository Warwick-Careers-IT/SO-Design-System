Twitter

Twitter is a good way to get up-to-date information from employers and organisations in your field of interest. In some sectors, it can be an excellent self-promotional tool. Many graduate recruiters and some SMEs will use their Twitter feed to share news, updates, deadlines and job opportunities.

Create a bio

  • If you want to ‘go public’ with your Twitter feed, then you need to think about your bio. Make sure your profile and avatar are both professional.
  • Think about your brand. It’s important to project a professional front but without sounding bland. Use your Twitter profile to encapsulate and reflect your aspirations, interests and personality.

Start following and searching

  • Follow recruiters and organisations to make sure you hear about job postings, news and initiatives.
  • Retweet relevant information, but don’t forget to add your own voice.
  • Use hashtags to help organise your job search.

Tweet!

  • This is one of the most immediate and powerful ways to connect with people and raise your profile. Get the balance right though – aim for quality, not quantity.
  • Be professional and think before you tweet.
