  • How work experience can help you

    Having some experience of work before you leave university has many benefits, more than you might first think.

  • Getting work experience on campus

    From the Arts Centre to the Academic Office, browse the campus experience pool to find paid and volunteer opportunities right here at Warwick.

  • Internships

    An internship is a structured, pre-defined period of work, with agreed goals and objectives, which sits outside of your studies.

  • Less common work experience options

    Use this section to get an overview of the popular ways of getting work experience, as well as some that you may not have considered.

  • Part time and temporary work

    Whilst we offer access to hundreds of vacancies on campus and via myAdvantage, you may also want to look elsewhere.

  • Placements

    A placement is a work experience that forms part of a degree, where you will often undertake an assignment, progress reports or other learning activities in addition to the tasks you perform within the role you have undertaken.

  • Researching your own work experience opportunities

    Whilst we offer access to hundreds of vacancies on campus and via myAdvantage, you may also want to look elsewhere.

  • Unpaid work

    Some employers offer the chance to work without pay, for the opportunity to learn about their business and gain experience.

  • Volunteering in the UK

    Volunteering offers great opportunities to get experience in diverse settings and occupations, working with local communities and diverse individuals

  • Volunteering overseas

    Volunteering overseas can be a life changing experience, providing an invaluable way of learning new skills and languages, experiencing new cultures, seeing new parts of the world, making friends and giving back to the country to which you are travelling.

  • Warwick Undergraduate Research Scholarship Scheme (URSS)

    Warwick Undergraduate Research Scholarship Scheme (URSS) offers bursary support for students undertaking a research project. For many students this is a great work experience opportunity, particularly if there are considering a career in research.

  • Warwick summer internships (WSI)

    120+ paid 6+ week experiences, exclusively for Warwick undergraduate students, with small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), charities and internal departments of the University
